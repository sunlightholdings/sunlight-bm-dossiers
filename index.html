How Sunlight works

Dossier architecture, evidence layers, and the engagement frame.

Sunlight builds case files the way a well-run firm would build them internally — sourced from public docket, regulatory, and corporate signal, layered with internal prototype scoring, and sharpened against a defense red-team before it ever reaches a partner's desk.

  1. 01

    Signal selection

    We start from a thesis, not a list. Each case is selected because the public record carries doctrinal authority, the pay-practice signal is visible, and the damages state improves leverage.

  2. 02

    Dossier architecture

    Every dossier carries six sections: thesis, doctrinal anchors, evidence layers, intake script, defense red-team, and a buyer-facing summary. Sections are written so a partner can read top-to-bottom in ten minutes.

  3. 03

    Evidence layers

    Public-record citations sit at the top layer. Internal prototype scoring (target score, class estimate, modeled gross) sits below, clearly marked as estimate. Plaintiff-intake pathways and worker-search queries sit deeper still, reserved until engagement.

  4. 04

    Carveout discipline

    For reclassification leads where a related case has already been filed, we deliver a carveout map: uncovered states, non-overlapping titles, later time periods, sister affiliates, and release-scope gaps. We do not resell filed cases.

  5. 05

    Verification required

    Internal prototype estimates and class-size figures are flagged as estimates. Named-plaintiff facts, paystubs, arbitration status, and PACER release-scope checks are gaps to be verified; every dossier names them explicitly.

  6. 06

    Engagement note

    If a dossier moves forward, Sunlight holds the sourcing pathway for one week while the firm reviews the full file and decides where it wants deeper diligence.
